Housing and Infrastructure Schemes

The Indian government has implemented various housing and infrastructure schemes. Some of the housing schemes include initiatives for rural, urban, and economically weaker sections (EWS) housing . Infrastructure schemes include the India Infrastructure Project Development Fund (IIPDF) and other innovative schemes aimed at promoting public-private partnerships (PPPs)  . There are also central government schemes for satellite towns and housing for all . The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development has introduced new schemes, including the building of 100 smart cities across India.

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is an initiative by the Government of India in which affordable housing will be provided to the urban poor with a target of building 2 crore (20 million) affordable houses by 31 March 2022. It has two components: Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (Urban) (PMAY-U) for the urban poor and Pradhan Mantri Awaas Yojana (Gramin) (PMAY-G and also PMAY-R) for the rural poor, the former administered by Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs and the latter by Ministry of Rural Development.

Mission Antyodaya

Adopted in Union Budget 2017-18, Mission Antyodaya is a convergence and accountability framework aiming to bring optimum use and management of resources allocated by 27 Ministries / Department of the Government of India under various programmes for the development of rural areas. It is envisaged as state-led initiative with Gram Panchayats as focal points of convergence efforts.

PMGSY

The Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY), was launched by the Govt. of India to provide connectivity to unconnected Habitations as part of a poverty reduction strategy. Govt. of India is endeavoring to set high and uniform technical and management standards and facilitating policy development and planning at State level in order to ensure sustainable management of the rural roads network.
